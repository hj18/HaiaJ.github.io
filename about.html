    • Bio
    Video games been my passion since I was a child, they helped me though many situation. Never thought I'll end up studying computer science, as a started my university jouney in Chemical Engineering but as time passed I wanted pursue a career in progamming/design because that's where my passion is.
    One of my favourite aspects of design is the ability the create new environments for players to explore and crafting spaces for gameplay and story, all these elements needs to complement each other for more enjoyable player experince.

    During university I studied many subjects such as 3D graphics and animation, this subject was really enjoyable as I got to design a scene in Unreal engine using models I made in Blender. This subject enhanced my Blender abilities but I also learned about lighting, positing and how it was come together to make a scene to tell a story.
    Recently I've been really interested in accessibility in video games which lead me to make it the topic of my dissertation. In dissertation I focus on settings and ways to help visually impaired players play games such as dungeon crawls.
    During my studies I managed to learn many skill, also many which I gained alone while joining extra activities such as game jams.
    Throughout my time is university I was given the opportnity to experiment and try out new software and techniques which lead me to gain some experince in using these softwares.
    It is easy for me to adapt to new software as throughout these few years since I started I tried out many such as Unity, Souce engine, Unreal and many more even with programming languages I've used many such as JavaScript, Ocaml, ARM, Java, C++ and many more.
